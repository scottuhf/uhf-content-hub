📋 Step 4

Content Style

The structural format — determines rhythm, viewer expectation, and how information is delivered.

Select all that apply

✓
🔬 Breakdown
Deep dive analysis — dissect a play, stat, or situation piece by piece.
✓
🔢 Countdown
Numbered list in descending order. Creates suspense — "and #1 is…"
✓
🏆 Ranking
Ordered tier list or comparative positioning. Sparks debate.
✓
😲 Reaction
Your live or recorded response to news, a clip, or a hot take.
✓
⚖️ This or That
Binary choice format. Forces audience to pick a side and comment.
✓
📉 Level of Concern
Rate how worried you are (1–10 scale). Relatable and shareable.
✓
🧠 Trivia
Quiz-style Q&A. Educates while entertaining. Great for retention.
✓
🎬 Skit
Short comedic or dramatic scene. Character-driven in under 60 seconds.
✓
💬 Interaction
Reply to a comment, duet, or stitch. Directly engages community.
✓
🎤 Ramble
Unstructured stream-of-consciousness. Raw and authentic.
✓
🎮 Game
Fun, nonsensical game to engage viewers on a topic.

🎣 Step 5

Hook

The first 1–3 seconds of your reel. Choose one or more tactics from any hook type, then write your opening line.

Select any tactics across all hook types

🔴 Verbal Hook — What you say ▾
The first words out of your mouth grab attention. No visual needed — your opening line is the hook.
✓
⚡ Shock
A stat, fact, or claim so surprising the viewer has to keep watching.
✓
🚫 Negative
Lead with what's wrong or what NOT to do. Controversy holds attention.
✓
❓ Question
Open with a question that makes the viewer want the answer.
✓
💥 Bold Claim
State an opinion so confidently it demands a response.
✓
📖 Story Open
Start mid-story. "So I'm watching film at 2am and I noticed something nobody else is talking about…"
✓
🤝 Relatability
Makes the viewer feel seen. "If you watched last night's game you already know…"
✓
